The opportunity

Latinos are everywhere in U.S. sports. The content isn't.

Across every major league — NFL, NBA, MLB, MLS, NHL — Latinos represent a massive and growing share of the fan base. But Spanish-language content barely exists. Most teams have zero social presence in Spanish. Most leagues produce one generic feed that ignores the cultural differences between Mexican, Colombian, Venezuelan, Argentine, and Central American audiences.

Halftime Media closes that gap with hyperlocal editorial, liveblogs, real-time coverage, and content designed for how this audience actually consumes media.

Why now

This isn't a trend. It's a tipping point.

Feb 2026 · Proof of concept
Bad Bunny's Super Bowl broke every record
First halftime show performed almost entirely in Spanish. 4.15 billion global views in 24 hours — the most-watched halftime show of all time. Telemundo peaked at 4.8M viewers, the highest Spanish-language Super Bowl audience ever. The demand is real.
2023–2026 · The Messi Effect
One player transformed an entire league
Inter Miami's revenue jumped 260% — from $56M to $200M. The 2025 MLS Cup drew 4.6M viewers, the youngest audience ever (70% under 45). The 2026 season opener packed 75,673 fans into the LA Coliseum. 9.7M tuned in on opening weekend. Messi proved the appetite for soccer content in the U.S. is massive — and overwhelmingly Latino.
Jun–Jul 2026
FIFA World Cup on U.S. soil
11 host cities, 35M+ Hispanics in those markets. The biggest sporting event in history — in Spanish-speaking neighborhoods. The organizations that build their content infrastructure now will own the audience after.
2025–2026
Leagues are going global — and Latino
The NFL played in Brazil and Madrid. The NBA is expanding its Spanish-language coverage. MLB is 30% Latino on the field but barely 11% in broadcast hours. Every league is waking up to this audience.
By 2035
Latinos will drive ⅓ of sports economy growth
$3.6 trillion in Latino buying power. McKinsey projects one-third of all U.S. sports economy growth will come from this demographic. The question isn't whether to invest — it's how fast.

Every month without a Spanish-language content strategy is a month your competitors get closer to this audience. We're ready to start today.
